[top] Temperature and molde fraction dependent model from sdevice

FILE: Default.tcl
EXPRESSION: 

Temperature dependent electron DOS is calculated using
Nc(x,T) = Nc300(x) * (T/300)^3/2
and temperature dependent electron DOS mass is calculated using
me/m0 = (Nc300/2.540e19)^2/3

CALCULATED PARAMETER: Nc300 returns room temperature electron DOS [cm^-3] me/m0 returns electron DOS mass [1]
VALIDITY:
REFERENCE:
Sentaurus Device User Guide
NOTES: Formula 2 is used to calculate the electron DOS

[top] Mole fraction and temperature dependent model from Levinshtein

FILE: Levinshtein-RT.tcl
EXPRESSION: 

Mole fraction dependent room temperature electron DOS mass is used to calculate the DOS using
Nc = 2*(2*pi*mn*k*T/h^2)^(3/2)

CALCULATED PARAMETER: mm returns mn/m0 [1]
VALIDITY: This model is valid under the assumption of temperature independent effective mass
REFERENCE:
"Handbook series on Semiconductor Parameters, Vol II" M. Levenshtein, S. Rumyantsev, M. Shur, World Scientific, 1999
NOTES: Formula 1 is used to calculate the electron DOS

[top] model from Piprek

FILE: Piprek.tcl
EXPRESSION: 

For conduction band with spherical isoenergy surface, electron DOS effective mass is given by
mn/m0 = me
For conduction band with ellipsoidal isoenergy surfaces, electron DOS effective mass is given by
mn/m0 = M^(2/3)*(mll*mt^2)^(1/3)
where mll = longitudinal effective mass, mt = transversal effective mass
and M = Number of equivalent conduction band minima
The electron DOS is calculated using
Nc = 2*(2*pi*kB*T*mn*m0/h^2)^(3/2)

CALCULATED PARAMETER: mm returns me/m0 [1]
VALIDITY: This model is valid at all temperatures assuming temperature independent DOS effective mass
REFERENCE:
"Semiconductor Optoelectronic Devices: Introduction to Physics and Simulation", J. Piprek, Elsevier Science(USA), 2003
NOTES: Low temperature electron DOS effective mass is used to calculate temperature dependent electron DOS. The electron DOS effective mass and DOS are calculated for the conduction band (Gamma, X or L) corresponding to the semiconductor bandgap. Formula 1 is used to calculate the electron DOS and DOS mass.
